Parents of American ISIS hostage Kayla Mueller 'hopeful' she's aliveThe parents of American Islamic State (ISIS) hostage Kayla Mueller, who the extremist group claims was killed Friday by a Jordanian airstrike on the Syrian city of Raqqa, are hopeful that their daughter is still alive."This news leaves us concerned, yet, we are still hopeful that Kayla is alive," Carl and Marsha Mueller said in a statement on Friday, according to multiplereports.Mueller's parents urged ISIS to contact them privately, and to treat their daughter as a guest. "You told us that you treated Kayla as your guest; as your guest, her safety and well-being remains your responsibility," they said.Mueller, a 26-year-old humanitarian aid worker, is believed to have been abducted on Aug. 4, 2013 in the Syrian city of Aleppo. She was on her way to a bus station, bound for Turkey, when she was taken, according to Doctors Without Borders.
