The Chancellor was a surprise guest at the opening of the football academy in Manchester, adjacent to the Premier League champion's home ground, reinforcing his commitment to boosting the region in an initiative to create a "Northern powerhouse" economy across the Pennines.Earlier, Kaldoon al-Mubarak, chairman of Manchester City Football Club, had told guests that the development of East Manchester in which Abu Dhabi and Sheikh Mansour have played a major role will continue.His remarks come amid uncertainty over the ongoing commitment of major investors and sovereign wealth funds in the oil-rich Gulf to continuing spending on so called "trophy assets" in light of falling prices for crude in recent months.
